What CCIR Provides
The Documentation Package Behind Every Citation
When CRI-H100 is cited in a credit agreement, CCIR supports the citation with:

Rate definition language — precise CRI benchmark rate definitions and methodology version references for use in credit agreement drafting.

Fallback language guidance — provisions addressing benchmark unavailability for counsel to adapt to the facility’s documentation standard.

Methodology documentation — published methodology and IOSCO compliance statement for due diligence packages and rating agency review.

Calculation Date packages — rate packages showing CRI-H100 and Generation Spread in a format designed for compliance certificate review.

Counsel is responsible for all operative agreement language. CCIR does not provide legal advice.

Who Engages CCIR
Built for transaction practitioners.

CCIR's services are designed for professionals working on active GPU-backed credit — not general market participants. The right contact is any party with a role in documenting, underwriting, or monitoring a GPU-collateralized facility.

Lenders
Credit Officers & Underwriters
Structuring GPU-backed term loans, revolvers, or equipment finance facilities where the collateral is H100, H200, or B200 infrastructure. Rate definition support and Calculation Date monitoring from underwriting through maturity.
Counsel
Structured Finance Attorneys
Drafting credit documentation that requires a named, independently verifiable reference rate in GPU collateral covenants. CCIR provides the rate definition, benchmark rate citation language, and Calculation Date packages.
Analysts
Credit Analysts & Rating Staff
Evaluating GPU-collateralized exposure — stress scenarios, collateral coverage analysis, generation obsolescence risk. CRI-H100 and the Generation Spread provide the independent data anchors for collateral and revenue quality analysis.
