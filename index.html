The Problem

GPU-Backed Credit Lacks a Reference Standard

GPU compute infrastructure is now pledged as collateral in direct lending facilities, securitizations, and equipment finance structures. Yet no independent, citable reference rate exists for GPU rental market prices — the primary cash flow driver and collateral value signal for these exposures.

No Benchmark
Credit agreements referencing “market rates” for GPU compute have no independent rate to cite. Lenders rely on counterparty quotes — the governance problem LIBOR reform was designed to solve.
No Tier Standard
GPU compute spans a five-tier price ladder from hyperscaler on-demand to secondary spot. No standard maps this structure or tracks migration velocity between tiers.
No Depreciation Curve
Generational transitions — H100 to H200 to B200 — compress rental yields faster than traditional equipment finance models assume. No published curves track this in real time.
No Independent Audit Trail
Without an append-only, hash-verified data series, there is no basis for pre-transaction audit attestations or post-event methodology verification in structured products.

CCIR was built specifically to fill this gap — an independent administrator with no commercial exposure to GPU rental markets, publishing under an open methodology designed for credit document citation from day one.

Analytical Framework

The Compute Yield Waterfall

GPU collateral value is a function of where a chip sits in the deployment tier ladder and how fast it migrates downward as new generations enter production. Unlike traditional equipment ABS — which assumes monotonic depreciation — GPU compute introduces a feedback mechanism: inference demand can re-rate older chips upward in rental yield terms, creating non-monotonic collateral value paths that static depreciation schedules cannot capture.

Stage 1 — Rental Yield
Tier Migration & Rate Depreciation
The decline in prevailing rental rates as a GPU model cascades from training tier through inference to commodity compute. CRI-H100 anchors the current training tier. Cross-tier spreads — B200/H100, H200/H100, H100/A100 — measure migration velocity and are the primary cash flow signals for GPU lending facilities.
Stage 2 — Tier Re-rating
Inference Demand as Re-rating Mechanism
As B200 and H200 displace H100 from training workloads, inference demand may absorb available H100 and A100 supply — re-rating their rental yields upward rather than allowing monotonic decline. The A100/L40S near-parity spread is the early signal: inference demand is already absorbing prior-generation supply ahead of static model expectations.
↕ The spread between tiers over time is the migration velocity signal — not captured by static depreciation schedules

Lenders using static depreciation curves are systematically mispricing GPU collateral in both directions: overstating risk when inference demand re-rates older chips, understating it when new generations accelerate cascade faster than scheduled. CRI tier spreads provide the empirical signal to detect both conditions in real time.

Full treatment in the working paper: The Compute Yield Waterfall Framework (CCIR Working Paper 2026-01, SSRN — forthcoming).

Independence

Benchmark Integrity and Structural Independence

The integrity of any financial benchmark depends on the structural independence of its administrator from the market the benchmark measures.

CCIR does not operate a GPU rental marketplace. CCIR has no commercial interest in GPU rental prices, transaction volume, or the financial performance of any marketplace operator. CCIR cannot benefit commercially from any particular CRI-H100 level.

CCIR does not accept submissions. CRI-H100 is calculated from publicly available API data with no panel banks, no contributor relationships, and no submitter conflicts. The governance challenge that produced LIBOR manipulation does not apply.

CCIR does not own GPU hardware or hold GPU-backed loans. CCIR has no inventory exposure to GPU collateral value and is not a GPU lender, lessor, or hardware broker.

The methodology is independently reproducible without CCIR’s cooperation. Any party can query the Vast.ai, Shadeform, and RunPod public APIs, apply the published filters, and reproduce any CRI-H100 value. No data license, terminal subscription, or CCIR relationship is required.

See Governance Framework §§3.1 and 3.5 for the full structural independence disclosure.
