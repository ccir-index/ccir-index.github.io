Analytical Framework

GPU collateral analysis requires tracking two distinct depreciation processes that move independently and respond to different market forces. CCIR's two-curve framework separates these explicitly — a distinction that is absent in most existing GPU lending underwriting.

Curve 1 — Rental Rate
Rental Rate Depreciation
The decline in the prevailing market rental rate for a GPU model over time, driven by new supply entering the market and improving price-per-performance of newer generations. CRI-H100 anchors this curve at the current-generation training tier. Additional tier indices — tracking H200 and inference-grade GPUs — will extend this curve as the pipeline expands. Rental rate depreciation affects cash flow projections in GPU lending facilities.
Curve 2 — Resale / Liquidation
Hardware Resale Depreciation
The decline in secondary-market liquidation value of the physical hardware. Driven by secondary market supply and demand, the physical asset's own depreciation, and generational transitions. This curve sets the collateral floor in a default scenario — distinct from, and not predictable from, the rental rate alone.
↕ The spread between the two curves is a measure of collateral risk not captured by either curve alone

Lenders who model only rental rate depreciation implicitly assume the liquidation floor moves in proportion to rental rates — an assumption that breaks down when secondary hardware market dynamics diverge from rental market conditions. The two-curve framework makes this assumption explicit and testable. CRI-H100 provides the empirical rental rate anchor; as additional tier indices are published, cross-tier spread dynamics will directly track workload migration from training toward inference and commodity compute. CRI-S, the planned companion index, will directly measure the spread between the rental and resale curves.

Full treatment in the working paper: The Compute Yield Waterfall Framework: Valuing GPU Compute Assets Across Deployment Tiers (CCIR Working Paper 2026-01, SSRN — forthcoming).

CRI-H100 is calculated from spot rental listings across multiple public marketplaces. CCIR also collects posted rate cards from hyperscalers and mid-market platforms — published alongside CRI-H100 as contextual data. The spread between these posted rates and CRI-H100 is the reliability premium: what the market charges for enterprise-grade GPU access over marginal spot supply.

Benchmark Integrity and Independence

The integrity of any financial benchmark depends on the structural independence of its administrator from the market the benchmark measures. CRI-H100 is designed around structural independence — a property that cannot be achieved through policy alone when a benchmark administrator has commercial exposure to the underlying asset.

CCIR does not operate a GPU rental marketplace. CCIR has no commercial interest in GPU rental prices, transaction volume, or the financial performance of any marketplace operator. CCIR cannot benefit commercially from any particular CRI-H100 level.

CCIR does not accept submissions. CRI-H100 is calculated from publicly available API data with no panel banks, no contributor relationships, and no submitter conflicts. The governance challenge that produced LIBOR manipulation does not apply.

CCIR does not own GPU hardware or hold GPU-backed loans. CCIR has no inventory exposure to GPU collateral value and is not a GPU lender, lessor, or hardware broker.

The methodology is independently reproducible without CCIR's cooperation. Any party can query the Vast.ai public API, apply the published filters, and reproduce any CRI-H100 value. No data license, terminal subscription, or CCIR relationship is required.

See Governance Framework §§3.1 and 3.5 for the full structural independence disclosure, including a due diligence checklist for evaluating competing benchmark administrators.

Index Family

CCIR will expand the index family as the pipeline matures — publishing the GPU models that best track tier migration and collateral dynamics, not a fixed predetermined list. Cross-tier spreads between training, inference, and commodity tiers are the signal; the indices are chosen to make that signal legible.

Index Tier Status Description
CRI-H100 Training Live Daily median H100 SXM rental rate, multi-source spot marketplace. Current-generation training-tier reference rate. First publication March 2026.
CRI-R Reference rates Live Companion series tracking posted rate cards from hyperscalers and mid-market platforms (Azure, AWS, GCP, RunPod). Commodity and HPC-premium tiers separated. Updated daily.
CRI-H200 / CRI-L40S Training / Inference Planned Next-gen and inference-tier indices. Cross-tier spread between H200, H100, and L40S tracks workload migration from training toward inference and commodity compute — core input to CRI-D.
CRI-S Cross-tier spread Planned Rental-resale spread index. Measures whether the GPU liquidation floor is holding relative to rental rate depreciation. Requires verified resale transaction data. Q3–Q4 2026.
CRI-D Depreciation curves Planned Cross-generational depreciation curves derived from CRI time series. Requires 12–18 months of data. Q1–Q2 2027.
Regional variants Geographic Roadmap EU and APAC variants under identical methodology. Enables geographic spread analysis for cross-border GPU lending facilities.
