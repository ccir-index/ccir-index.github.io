The Problem

The GPU-backed lending market exceeds $20 billion in outstanding obligations. Lenders need a neutral, independently verifiable rental rate reference to underwrite collateral, set covenant triggers, and stress-test portfolios. That reference did not previously exist to the governance standard required for credit document citation.

Volatile markets
H100 rental rates have declined 60–70% in 18 months. Depreciation assumptions vary by a factor of six across market participants.
Proprietary indices
Existing rental rate indices are administered by entities with marketplace or trading interests — methodology cannot be independently reproduced or audited.
No governance standard
Post-LIBOR, benchmarks cited in credit documents require transparent methodology, independent reproducibility, and formal change control. None existed for GPU compute.
Lenders abstaining
A non-trivial share of institutional lenders have declined to participate in GPU-backed lending, citing inability to develop defensible collateral valuation methodologies.

CRI-H100 — Current Status

CRI-H100 is a weekly index measuring the trailing 7-day median $/GPU-hour for NVIDIA H100 SXM on-demand rental listings on the US Vast.ai marketplace. Calculated from publicly available data. Independently verifiable by any third party. Governed with reference to the IOSCO Principles for Financial Benchmarks.

Burn-in Period
Data collection commenced February 26, 2026. Daily snapshots archived across 9 GPU models. First published index value: March 5, 2026.

Benchmark Integrity and Independence

The integrity of any financial benchmark depends on the structural independence of its administrator from the market the benchmark measures. CRI-H100 is designed around structural independence — a property that cannot be achieved through policy alone when a benchmark administrator has commercial exposure to the underlying asset.

CCIR does not operate a GPU rental marketplace. CCIR has no commercial interest in GPU rental prices, transaction volume, or the financial performance of any marketplace operator. CCIR cannot benefit commercially from any particular CRI-H100 level.

CCIR does not accept submissions. CRI-H100 is calculated from publicly available API data with no panel banks, no contributor relationships, and no submitter conflicts. The governance challenge that produced LIBOR manipulation does not apply.

CCIR does not own GPU hardware or hold GPU-backed loans. CCIR has no inventory exposure to GPU collateral value and is not a GPU lender, lessor, or hardware broker.

The methodology is independently reproducible without CCIR's cooperation. Any party can query the Vast.ai public API, apply the published filters, and reproduce any CRI-H100 value. No data license, terminal subscription, or CCIR relationship is required.

See Governance Framework §§3.1 and 3.5 for the full structural independence disclosure, including a due diligence checklist for evaluating competing benchmark administrators.
