Where Current Documentation Falls Short
Three gaps that leave lenders exposed on active GPU-backed facilities.
01
Collateral marked to the past, not the market

H100 on-demand asking prices fell more than 60% in 18 months. A lender without a live market reference rate had no covenant signal until that move had already happened.

02
No trigger for the transition already underway

H100 collateral was underwritten as training hardware — and training contracts carry materially less revenue volatility than inference. As Blackwell displaces Hopper for large-model training, a borrower’s revenue mix can shift from contracted and sticky to spot-adjacent and elastic. A lender monitoring utilization figures has no line of sight to that migration until it shows up as a coverage problem. A revenue coverage test anchored to CRI-H100 is sensitive to that shift without requiring the borrower to report it — contracted revenue converging toward the on-demand spot rate is the signal.

03
GPU collateral moves faster than the documentation was built for

A 36-month facility spans at least one full GPU generation transition — a foreseeable event, not a tail risk. When B200 spot prices converge with H100, the training premium justifying H100 collateral valuations is effectively arbitraged away. No current deal structure has a mechanism to respond to it as it happens.

Illustrative Reference Rate Applications
01
Revenue Coverage Test
CRI-H100 may be cited as the independent market floor against which contracted GPU revenue is measured — at a coverage multiple set by counsel and lenders at underwriting. A Rate Cap prevents technical breach when the spot market rises above a borrower’s contracted rate.
02
Borrowing Base Reference Rate
CRI-H100 may be cited as the market rate in a monthly borrowing base formula, marking GPU collateral to the live on-demand market rather than book depreciation. Utilization factors and advance rates are set by counsel and lenders at closing.
03
Generation Spread Signal
The Generation Spread — (CRI-B200 − CRI-H100) ÷ CRI-H100 — is an observable proxy for H100 obsolescence risk. When B200 supply expands and its price falls toward H100 levels, the spread compresses. CCIR publishes both rates daily; counsel and lenders determine what spread thresholds mean for the deal.

Generation Spread — Live Signal

A wide spread reflects B200 priced above H100 in the on-demand market. Spread compression — B200 prices falling toward H100 levels — reduces the price incentive to choose H100 over next-generation hardware. The H100/A100 spread shows the same dynamic on a longer horizon: where H100 is headed as prior-generation capacity absorbs into the inference market.
