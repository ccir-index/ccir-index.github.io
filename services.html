Market Price Monitoring Covenant
Market Price Monitoring — Any GPU Facility
The lowest-friction CRI citation — no financial consequence.
Profile-agnostic surveillance covenant. Two disclosure thresholds anchored to CRI-8G at closing. No prepayment trigger, no formula adjustment, no Event of Default. Compatible with any GPU-backed credit agreement regardless of deal structure.
The Market Price Monitoring covenant requires the borrower to deliver a notice when CRI-8G for the pledged GPU model falls below defined thresholds relative to the rate at closing. The thresholds are set at closing and do not change. The only consequence is disclosure — what the borrower does with the information is a business decision, not a covenant event.
Threshold Trigger Consequence
Level 1 CRI-8G falls below 75% of CRI-8G at closing for two consecutive Calculation Dates Market Price Notice — borrower delivers written notice within 5 business days
Level 2 CRI-8G falls below 50% of CRI-8G at closing for two consecutive Calculation Dates Market Price Alert — borrower delivers enhanced notice including fleet utilization and re-pricing impact assessment
Illustrative Example
Closing CRI-8G (H100): $2.50/GPU/hr
Level 1 trigger: CRI-8G < $1.875 for two consecutive dates
Level 2 trigger: CRI-8G < $1.250 for two consecutive dates

If CRI-8G publishes at $1.80 on two consecutive Calculation Dates, a Level 1 Market Price Notice is triggered. The borrower delivers notice. No financial consequence follows. The lender now has a documented data point for portfolio monitoring — and the borrower has a documented record of timely disclosure.
