How CRI Rates Are Referenced
in Credit Agreements

GPU-backed credit agreements need an independent, named market rate — one that neither party controls and any counterparty can verify. These illustrative examples show how CRI-H100 may be cited as a reference rate in revenue coverage tests, market monitoring covenants, and borrowing base formulas. All parameters and thresholds are set by counsel and lenders. CCIR publishes the rate.

Why GPU Credit Agreements Need a Named Reference Rate
Market Rate vs. Book Value
Book depreciation does not move with the rental market
60–70%
H100 rental rate decline, early 2024 → late 2025

H100 on-demand asking prices fell more than 60% in 18 months. Revenue covenants and borrowing base formulas that reference the live on-demand market need an independent, named rate to anchor those calculations — one both parties can verify independently.

Independent Reference Rate
A rate defined by either party creates documentation risk
No standard
No independent GPU compute reference rate in current deal documentation

A rate defined by the lender favors the lender. A rate defined by the borrower favors the borrower. Syndicated facilities need a rate all lenders can independently verify. Post-LIBOR, compliance functions have developed institutional resistance to reference rates that lack independent governance and published methodology. CRI-H100 is structured to meet that standard.

Rate Deterioration Visibility
Quarterly calculation dates create blind windows between signals

When on-demand spot rates begin to fall, quarterly financial covenants leave 60–90 days between the signal and the lender’s next checkpoint. A market monitoring covenant that references CRI-H100 daily can surface rate deterioration as it happens — enabling borrower disclosure and increased surveillance frequency before the decline compounds into a financial covenant trigger.

Monthly Borrowing Base
Collateral certification needs an independently verifiable rate

A monthly borrowing base certificate marking the GPU fleet to the on-demand market requires a rate neither party controls. Without a named independent reference rate, borrowing base availability is either frozen to closing assumptions or calculated from a number that cannot be independently reproduced — creating exactly the interpretive friction that surfaces in workouts. CRI-H100 is published daily and reproducible by any party with access to the underlying sources.

Illustrative Applications of CRI-H100 as a Reference Rate
Application A
Revenue Coverage Test
A revenue maintenance test may cite CRI-H100 as the independent market floor against which contracted GPU revenue is measured — at a coverage multiple negotiated by counsel and lenders at underwriting. A Rate Cap provision protects a performing borrower from technical breach when the spot market rises above their contracted rate. Threshold levels are credit judgments; CRI-H100 is the observable market anchor they reference.
CRI-H100 cited by name
Application B
Market Price Monitoring
A surveillance covenant may reference CRI-H100 as the independently verifiable market signal for rental rate deterioration — triggering borrower disclosure and increased calculation frequency when the rate declines materially from the Closing Date Rate. Decline thresholds are set by counsel and lenders based on the facility’s LTV cushion and contract coverage at origination. Not a financial trigger; increases lender visibility before a coverage breach materializes.
Surveillance · no financial trigger
Application C
Borrowing Base Reference Rate
A monthly borrowing base formula may cite CRI-H100 as the market rate used to calculate eligible GPU collateral value — at utilization and advance rate assumptions negotiated by counsel and lenders at closing. The formula marks the fleet to the live on-demand market monthly rather than to book depreciation or the borrower’s contracted revenue. All parameters other than CRI-H100 are fixed at closing; CRI-H100 is the single observable input that updates each period.
Monthly mark · independent daily rate
Application D
Generation Spread Surveillance
A surveillance covenant may cite the Generation Spread — (CRI-B200 − CRI-H100) ÷ CRI-H100 — as an observable proxy for H100 collateral obsolescence risk. When B200 prices converge toward H100 levels, the spread compresses. Counsel and lenders determine what spread level triggers borrower notice requirements and whether subsequent adjustments to borrowing base parameters are warranted. CCIR publishes both rates daily.
CRI-B200 & CRI-H100 cited together

On workload composition A revenue coverage test anchored to CRI-H100 is sensitive to workload composition without requiring the borrower to report it. Training revenue sits materially above the on-demand spot rate; inference revenue competes with it. A borrower whose contracted GPU revenue is converging toward CRI-H100 may be migrating toward lower-value workloads — the coverage test catches that compression as it happens, because the rate it measures against is live and independent.

The key asymmetry: a declining spot rate makes the Revenue Coverage Test easier to pass (locked contracts look better relative to a falling floor) while simultaneously reducing Borrowing Base availability (the fleet marks down against outstanding principal). Lenders must monitor both together. The final scenario shows the compound risk when contract protection expires into a lower market — the same rate decline that was previously absorbed by locked contracts now flows directly into the coverage test.
